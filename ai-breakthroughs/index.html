Timeline

Recent Breakthroughs

Toolformer: Language Models Can Teach Themselves to Use Tools
major 2023 · Timo Schick et al.
Toolformer proved LLMs can self-teach tool use, enabling them to overcome inherent limitations by calling external APIs when needed.
Hierarchical Text-Conditional Image Generation with CLIP Latents
major 2022 · Aditya Ramesh et al.
DALL-E 2 demonstrated that diffusion models conditioned on CLIP embeddings can generate high-quality images from text, making AI art commercially viable.
Training Compute-Optimal Large Language Models
major 2022 · Jordan Hoffmann et al.
Chinchilla established that models should be trained on far more data than previously assumed, rebalancing the compute-optimal frontier for LLMs.
Training language models to follow instructions with human feedback
paradigm-shift 2022 · Long Ouyang et al.
InstructGPT showed RLHF can align LLMs to human intent, making a small aligned model preferred over a much larger unaligned one.
Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models
major 2020 · Jared Kaplan et al.
Established that language model performance follows predictable power laws with scale, making the case for dramatically larger models.
