Can multi-layer neural networks be trained effectively with gradient descent?

Context

After Minsky and Papert showed single-layer limits, the key question became whether deeper networks (which theoretically could solve non-linear problems) could actually be trained. The credit assignment problem — how to update weights in hidden layers — was the core challenge.

Breakthroughs

Learning representations by back-propagating errors
paradigm-shift 1986 · David Rumelhart et al. · Nature
Backpropagation made multi-layer neural networks trainable, solving the credit assignment problem and launching the connectionism revival.
