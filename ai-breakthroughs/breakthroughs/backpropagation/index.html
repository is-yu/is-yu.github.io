paradigm-shift 1986 Nature

Learning representations by back-propagating errors

David Rumelhart, Geoffrey Hinton, Ronald Williams

Summary

Backpropagation made multi-layer neural networks trainable, solving the credit assignment problem and launching the connectionism revival.

Key Findings

Finding 1
The backpropagation algorithm can efficiently compute gradients for all weights in a multi-layer network using the chain rule, enabling training of networks with hidden layers.
Solved the credit assignment problem, making deep networks trainable and reviving neural network research after the first AI winter.
Finding 2
Multi-layer networks trained with backprop can learn distributed representations that capture abstract features of the input.
Demonstrated that learned internal representations emerge naturally from end-to-end training.

Context

While backpropagation was independently discovered multiple times (Werbos 1974, Parker 1985), Rumelhart, Hinton and Williams's 1986 Nature paper demonstrated it convincingly and catalyzed the connectionism revival.
