paradigm-shift 1969 MIT Press

Perceptrons: An Introduction to Computational Geometry

Marvin Minsky, Seymour Papert

Summary

Proved single-layer perceptrons cannot solve non-linear problems, contributing to the first AI winter but also motivating multi-layer research.

Key Findings

Finding 1
Single-layer perceptrons cannot compute XOR or any non-linearly separable function. They are limited to linear decision boundaries.
Formally established the computational limits of single-layer networks, triggering the first AI winter for neural network research.

Context

The enthusiasm around perceptrons led to inflated expectations. Minsky and Papert's rigorous analysis showed fundamental limitations, which many interpreted (incorrectly) as applying to all neural networks.
