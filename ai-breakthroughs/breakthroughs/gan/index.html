paradigm-shift 2014 NeurIPS 2014

Generative Adversarial Nets

Ian Goodfellow, Jean Pouget-Abadie, Mehdi Mirza, Bing Xu, David Warde-Farley, Sherjil Ozair, Aaron Courville, Yoshua Bengio

Summary

GANs proved neural networks can generate realistic novel images through adversarial training, creating a new paradigm for generative modeling.

Key Findings

Finding 1
A generator and discriminator network trained adversarially can learn to produce realistic samples from the data distribution without explicit density estimation.
Introduced a fundamentally new training paradigm for generative models that spawned an entire subfield of research.

Context

Prior generative approaches (VAEs, Boltzmann machines) required explicit likelihood computation. GANs bypassed this with game-theoretic training, achieving sharper outputs at the cost of training instability.
