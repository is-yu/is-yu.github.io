major 2022 NeurIPS 2022

Training Compute-Optimal Large Language Models

Jordan Hoffmann, Sebastian Borgeaud, Arthur Mensch, Elena Buchatskaya, Trevor Cai, Eliza Rutherford, Diego de Las Casas, Lisa Anne Hendricks, Johannes Welbl, Aidan Clark, Tom Hennigan, Eric Noland, Katie Millican, George van den Driessche, Bogdan Damoc, Aurelia Guy, Simon Osindero, Karen Simonyan, Erich Elsen, Jack Rae, Oriol Vinyals, Laurent Sifre

Summary

Chinchilla established that models should be trained on far more data than previously assumed, rebalancing the compute-optimal frontier for LLMs.

Key Findings

Finding 1
Current large language models are significantly over-parameterized and under-trained. For compute-optimal training, model size and training data should be scaled equally.
Showed that a 70B model trained on 4x more data outperforms a 280B model, fundamentally changing how compute budgets are allocated.

Context

The Chinchilla scaling laws corrected Kaplan et al.'s recommendation to prioritize model size. This had immediate practical impact — subsequent models (LLaMA, etc.) adopted compute-optimal ratios.
