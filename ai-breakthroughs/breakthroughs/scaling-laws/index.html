major 2020 arXiv

Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models

Jared Kaplan, Sam McCandlish, Tom Henighan, Tom Brown, Benjamin Chess, Rewon Child, Scott Gray, Alec Radford, Jeffrey Wu, Dario Amodei

Summary

Established that language model performance follows predictable power laws with scale, making the case for dramatically larger models.

Key Findings

Finding 1
Language model performance follows smooth power-law relationships with model size, dataset size, and compute budget, spanning many orders of magnitude with remarkable predictability.
Made scaling a principled engineering decision rather than guesswork, enabling rational allocation of billions in compute investment.
Finding 2
Performance depends most strongly on scale (compute, data, parameters) and is surprisingly insensitive to architectural details within the Transformer family.
Shifted research focus from architecture search to scaling methodology.

Context

These laws were empirically observed at OpenAI and gave quantitative predictions for how much larger models would improve. This directly motivated the GPT-3 training run.
