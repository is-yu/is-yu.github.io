BERT: Pre-training of Deep Bidirectional Transformers for Language Understanding

Jacob Devlin, Ming-Wei Chang, Kenton Lee, Kristina Toutanova

Summary

BERT showed bidirectional pre-training produces superior language understanding, making a single model competitive across all NLP benchmarks.

Key Findings

Finding 1
Bidirectional pre-training with masked language modeling achieves state-of-the-art on 11 NLP benchmarks simultaneously, surpassing task-specific architectures.
Proved that a single pre-trained model can be the best approach for virtually all NLP tasks, obsoleting task-specific architectures.

Context

BERT's key insight was bidirectional context — unlike GPT's left-to-right pre-training, BERT's masked LM objective sees context from both directions. This was particularly effective for understanding tasks.
