paradigm-shift 2012 NeurIPS 2012

ImageNet Classification with Deep Convolutional Neural Networks

Alex Krizhevsky, Ilya Sutskever, Geoffrey Hinton

Summary

AlexNet proved deep CNNs learn hierarchical visual representations far superior to hand-engineered features, launching the deep learning revolution.

Key Findings

Finding 1
A deep CNN trained end-to-end on ImageNet achieved a top-5 error rate of 15.3%, dramatically outperforming the second-place hand-engineered approach (26.2%).
Demonstrated that learned hierarchical representations vastly outperform hand-crafted features at scale.
Finding 2
ReLU activations and GPU training enabled practical training of deep convolutional networks on large datasets.
Introduced key engineering practices that enabled the deep learning revolution.

Context

Prior to AlexNet, computer vision relied on hand-designed features like SIFT and HOG. The ImageNet Large Scale Visual Recognition Challenge provided the benchmark that made this comparison definitive.
