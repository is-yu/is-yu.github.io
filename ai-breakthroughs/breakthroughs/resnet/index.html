paradigm-shift 2015 CVPR 2016

Deep Residual Learning for Image Recognition

Kaiming He, Xiangyu Zhang, Shaoqing Ren, Jian Sun

Summary

ResNet introduced skip connections enabling training of 100+ layer networks, solving the depth degradation problem and becoming a ubiquitous building block.

Key Findings

Finding 1
Skip connections (residual connections) allow training of networks with 152+ layers by letting gradients flow directly through shortcut paths, eliminating the degradation problem.
Enabled a new regime of network depth, with immediate improvements across all vision benchmarks and influence across all of deep learning.

Context

The degradation problem was distinct from vanishing gradients — even with batch normalization, deeper networks performed worse. ResNet's insight was that learning residual functions is easier than learning unreferenced mappings.
