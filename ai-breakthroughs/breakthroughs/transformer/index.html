paradigm-shift 2017 NeurIPS 2017

Attention Is All You Need

Ashish Vaswani, Noam Shazeer, Niki Parmar, Jakob Uszkoreit, Llion Jones, Aidan Gomez, Lukasz Kaiser, Illia Polosukhin

Summary

The Transformer replaced RNNs with self-attention for fully parallel sequence processing, becoming the foundation for all modern large language models.

Key Findings

Finding 1
The Transformer architecture uses self-attention to process all positions in parallel, replacing recurrence entirely while achieving state-of-the-art translation quality.
Enabled massively parallel training on sequences, removing the sequential bottleneck that limited RNN scaling.
Finding 2
Multi-head attention allows the model to jointly attend to information from different representation subspaces at different positions.
Created a flexible mechanism for modeling diverse relationships in data that proved universal across domains.

Context

Attention mechanisms existed before (Bahdanau 2014), but always alongside RNNs. The Transformer showed attention alone was sufficient, enabling the scaling that would lead to GPT, BERT, and all foundation models.
