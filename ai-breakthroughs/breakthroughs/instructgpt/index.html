paradigm-shift 2022 NeurIPS 2022

Training language models to follow instructions with human feedback

Long Ouyang, Jeffrey Wu, Xu Jiang, Diogo Almeida, Carroll Wainwright, Pamela Mishkin, Chong Zhang, Sandhini Agarwal, Katarina Slama, Alex Ray, John Schulman, Jacob Hilton, Fraser Kelton, Luke Miller, Maddie Simens, Amanda Askell, Peter Welinder, Paul Christiano, Jan Leike, Ryan Lowe

Summary

InstructGPT showed RLHF can align LLMs to human intent, making a small aligned model preferred over a much larger unaligned one.

Key Findings

Finding 1
RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback) using a reward model trained on human preference comparisons dramatically improves instruction following. A 1.3B InstructGPT model was preferred over a 175B GPT-3.
Proved that alignment techniques can make smaller models more useful than larger unaligned ones, establishing RLHF as the standard post-training method.

Context

InstructGPT built on earlier RLHF work (Christiano et al. 2017) and directly enabled ChatGPT, which brought LLMs to mainstream adoption.
