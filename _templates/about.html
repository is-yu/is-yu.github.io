About This Project

What Is This?

AI Breakthroughs is a living research survey that organizes the history of artificial intelligence around the core questions that breakthrough findings answered or validated.

Rather than listing papers chronologically, we structure AI history as a series of questions that researchers asked — and the breakthroughs that answered them. Open questions track the latest research progress with pointers to the original papers.

How It Works

Data curation: New papers are ingested from arXiv, analyzed by the Claude API to extract key claims and map them to existing questions, then reviewed by a human curator before being published.

Content structure: Each question has a status (Answered, Open, or Partially Answered), linked breakthroughs, and progress updates. Breakthroughs link back to the questions they address and forward to new questions they raise.

Technology: This is a static site generated from YAML data files. The curation pipeline reuses components from arxiv-sanity-preserver for arXiv paper ingestion.
